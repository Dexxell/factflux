Science

Water Expands When Frozen: Unlike most substances, water expands by about 9% when it freezes, making ice less dense than liquid water. This is why ice floats on water. The Sun is Mostly Hydrogen: Around 75% of the Sun's mass is hydrogen, which fuels its nuclear fusion reactions, producing the energy that supports life on Earth. DNA is Surprisingly Long: If you stretched out all the DNA in a single human cell, it would measure about 2 meters (6.5 feet) long!

History

The Great Wall of China: The wall stretches over 13,000 miles and was built across centuries to protect China from invasions, particularly from Mongols. Cleopatra Wasn't Egyptian: Cleopatra, one of ancient Egypt's most famous rulers, was actually of Macedonian Greek descent, part of the Ptolemaic dynasty. The Black Death: This plague, caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis, killed an estimated 25–30 million people in Europe between 1347 and 1351, wiping out about a third of the population.
